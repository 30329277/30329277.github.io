Key Features

Everything you need for on-site weld quality inspection — from image capture to standards-based evaluation.

📷 Camera & Photo Import

Capture weld photos directly in-app with up to 20× zoom and precise focus control. Import existing images from your photo library. Background removal isolates the weld for cleaner measurement.

📐 Calibrated Measurement

Set a known reference length to calibrate the image, then measure any weld dimension on-screen. Supports linear distance measurement with pixel-to-millimeter conversion.

🔍 Edge Detection

Built-in Canny edge detection highlights weld boundaries automatically. Adjustable threshold and overlay help identify fusion lines, undercut edges, and reinforcement profiles.

📏 EN ISO 5817 Evaluation

Select the imperfection type (excess weld metal, undercut, misalignment, etc.) and the app evaluates your measurement against quality levels B, C, and D — with pass/fail results shown instantly.

📋 Project Management

Organize inspections into projects with metadata (weld number, location, welder ID). Store multiple measurements per project. All data persists locally using SwiftData.

🛠 Pipe Fittings Library

Built-in reference library for standard pipe fittings (elbows, tees, reducers) with dimensions and specifications — a handy reference during field inspections.

How It Works

Four simple steps from photo to evaluation report.

1. Capture

Use the built-in camera or import a photo from your library. Position the weld in frame and capture at high resolution. Use zoom for detail shots of imperfections.

2. Calibrate

Place a calibration reference (ruler, gauge) in the image. Draw a line across the known length and enter the real-world dimension. The app calculates the pixel-to-mm ratio for accurate measurement.

3. Measure

Measure weld dimensions directly on the photo — reinforcement height, undercut depth, misalignment offset, throat thickness, and more. Rotate and zoom freely while measuring.

4. Evaluate

Select the imperfection type and enter wall thickness. The app compares your measurement against EN ISO 5817 limits and shows the quality level achieved (B, C, D, or non-conforming).

FAQ

Does the app require an internet connection?

No. All features — camera, measurement, edge detection, and EN ISO 5817 evaluation — work fully offline.

Does the app collect any data?

No. Zero data collection. All photos and measurements stay on your device. See our Privacy Policy.

What standards does it support?

WeldSight evaluates weld imperfections against EN ISO 5817, covering quality levels B (stringent), C (intermediate), and D (moderate).

How accurate is the measurement?

Accuracy depends on your calibration reference and image quality. With a clearly visible ruler or gauge block in the photo, sub-millimeter precision is achievable.

Does it work on iPad?

Yes. WeldSight is a universal app that runs on both iPhone and iPad.

Can I export my measurements?

Yes. Measurement results and project data can be exported as reports for documentation and record-keeping.
